There was a season when every morning felt like a race I was already losing.
Coffee in one hand, notifications in the other, and no memory of what I actually wanted from the day.

![Sunlit morning journal](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1455390582262-044cdead277a?auto=format&fit=crop&w=1400&q=80)

I started small. Ten quiet minutes before opening my phone. A window cracked open. One page of writing with no agenda.

> You don't need a new life overnight. You need one honest morning repeated often.

#### Step One: Clear the Visual Noise

I removed everything from my bedside table except a lamp, a notebook, and a glass of water.
The room looked empty at first, then it looked calm.

#### Step Two: Anchor the First Hour

The first hour now has three simple actions: water, stretch, write.
No productivity app, no timer, no pressure.

#### Step Three: Protect the Mood

Before leaving home, I ask one question: _What do I want to feel by tonight?_
That answer usually guides the rest of the day better than any task list.
