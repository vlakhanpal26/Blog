I landed in Paris with one carry-on and no expectations.
By Sunday evening, I had gone on three very different dates and learned more than I expected.

#### 1. The Rooftop Dinner

He arrived with flowers, spoke in full paragraphs, and listened closely.
It was objectively lovely, but I felt myself performing instead of relaxing.

![Dinner table in Paris](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1517248135467-4c7edcad34c4?auto=format&fit=crop&w=1400&q=80)

#### 2. The Bookstore Walk

We met at a tiny English bookstore near the river and wandered for hours.
No grand gestures, just curiosity. That date reminded me that comfort can be more magnetic than intensity.

> Chemistry is exciting. Compatibility is sustainable.

#### 3. The Last-Minute Coffee

This one was spontaneous, awkward, and refreshingly honest.
We both admitted we were still figuring things out, and somehow that made the conversation easier.

By the end of the trip, I stopped asking, "Did they like me?" and started asking, "Did I feel like myself?"
